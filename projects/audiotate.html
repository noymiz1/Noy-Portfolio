Audiotate

Designing a Cross-Platform Music Annotation Experience

Product Design UI/UX Digital Platform 2021

Overview

Audiotate is a music annotation digital platform created in Harvard University's Design for Desirability course. It allows users to capture and revisit music as memories, turning passive listening into active memory-making across all major streaming services.

The platform includes features like personal lyric annotations, song-based journaling, customizable tags, and cross-platform playlist organization. My contribution focused on developing the app UI design and establishing the distinctive purple-to-orange gradient visual identity that defines the brand.

Research & Strategy

Our team conducted user interviews with music enthusiasts across different age groups to understand how they form emotional connections with songs and what drives them to share music experiences with others.

We created user personas, including "Social Sara," who values sharing songs with friends and attaching personal context to her favorite tracks.

Through our research, we identified a clear opportunity for a platform that treats songs as memory vessels rather than just consumption items. Users wanted a "song-based diary" where they could document their relationship with music over time.

User persona highlighting needs for cross-platform music annotation and sharing

Problem Statement

Platform Fragmentation

Users listen to music across Spotify, Apple Music, and SoundCloud but lack a unified way to organize and annotate their music.

Limited Personalization

Current platforms offer minimal ways to connect personal memories and emotions to specific songs or lyrics.

Social Music Experience

Existing services don't support meaningful sharing of personal music experiences and annotations with friends.

Design Process

01

Brand Identity Development

Created a distinctive visual identity with a purple-to-orange gradient color scheme that evokes emotion and energy. The spring/coil logo represents both musical notes and the layered nature of personal annotations.

02

User Interface Design

Developed a social feed-based interface that showcases annotations from friends while maintaining clear pathways to personal annotation features. Used multiple color themes to distinguish between content types.

Solution

Social Music Feed

A dynamic feed that displays annotations and notes from friends and the wider Audiotate community, allowing users to discover new music through personal connections.

Song-Based Journaling

Users can create personal journals attached to songs, documenting when and how they discovered tracks, what they mean, and how their relationship with them evolves over time.

Explore Page

A unified music library that brings together tracks from Spotify, Apple Music, and SoundCloud, with consistent annotation features across all platforms.

Key Features

Audiotate transforms how users interact with music by introducing several innovative features:

The social feed allows users to see, like, comment, and bookmark song annotations from their network

Users can tag songs with custom categories, making it easy to create mood-based collections that work across streaming platforms. The tagging system helps users quickly find music that matches their current emotional state.

The lyric annotation feature allows users to highlight and attach personal notes to specific lines in songs, creating a deeper connection with the music and enabling more meaningful sharing with friends.

Reflection & Learnings

This project underscored the importance of designing for emotional engagement rather than just functional efficiency. Music is deeply personal, and creating tools that honor that relationship required balancing technical capabilities with emotional touchpoints.

The visual design system—using gradients that transition from purple to orange—successfully created an emotionally resonant interface that users found inviting and engaging, proving that aesthetic choices significantly impact how users connect with a product.

Team & Collaboration

Audiotate was created in collaboration as part of Harvard University's Design for Desirability course. My contribution focused on the app UI design screens and establishing the distinctive visual identity with its signature purple-to-orange gradient and spring/coil logo that embodies the concept of "Music as Memories."
